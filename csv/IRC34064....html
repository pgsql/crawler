Detailed Description

The position reports to the Director of Benefits in Jersey City, NJ; and is responsible for managing and planning the day-to-day administration of the E*Trade Financial employee benefits programs – including, but not limited to: group health, 401(k) retirement plan, non-qualified deferred compensation plan, short-term and long-term disability, life and accident insurance, tuition reimbursement, employee relocation, flexible spending plan, paid time off, voluntary benefits,  and a  working knowledge in corporate Wellness programs. The candidate must also have a strong working knowledge of all pertinent federal and local regulations, filing and compliance requirements (both adopted and pending) affecting employee benefit programs including: ERISA, COBRA, FMLA, ADA, HIPAA, Section 125, workers compensation, Medicare, social security and DOL requirements. 

The role requires:

- Understanding of the sensitive nature of information; and the ability to navigate complex situations and tasks in a discrete, effective and efficient manner.

-  Strong proven analytical skills and experience updating and utilizing Human Resources Information Systems (HRIS), preferably Oracle.

 
Job Requirements

Primary Responsibilities:

Benefit Plan Compliance

- Ensure compliance with all pertinent federal and local regulations, filing and compliance requirements affecting employee benefit programs including ERISA, COBRA, FMLA, ADA, Section 125, Workers Compensation, Medicare, Social Security and DOL requirements.  Submit compliance summary reports as required.  Maintain knowledge of industry trends and legislation.

- Partner with outside consultants, procurement department, and legal counsel to prepare and execute benefit documentation such as original and amended plan texts, benefit agreements, insurance policies and required amendments to vendor contracts. Instruct insurance carriers, trustees, and other administrative agencies outside the company to effect changes in benefit program.

- Create and maintain individual employee and general benefits files. Log and track the placement of file contents, monitoring access to files. Ensure key document retention compliance requirements are met as per government regulations, Sarbanes –Oxley requirements, and internal department guidelines.

Retirement Plan Administration

- Manage the administration of employee retirement plans, Act as liaison with retirement plan record keeper/trustee, external auditors and legal counsel.  Maintain files of correspondence, reports, and forms.

- Communicate with 401(k) active and non-active participants about plan provisions as required. 

- Partner with Payroll to ensure proper deduction and funding of all employee and employer retirement plan contributions.

- Coordinate the annual 401(k) external audit and 5500 reporting process for all insurance and 401(k) plans that require reporting to the IRS.  Contact vendors for accurate information. Work closely with outside counsel, review the reports for accuracy and ensure they are sent to the IRS in a timely manner.  Work closely with the auditors to support their needs during the annual 401(k) audit process.

Benefits Administration

- Independently administer the daily aspects of E*Trade’s  benefit programs, including but not limited to: the maintenance of medical, dental, disability, life insurance, FMLA, flexible spending account COBRA, tuition reimbursement, and employee relocation records.  Responsibilities include: funding and reconciling vendor invoices, verifying eligibility counts, calculating costs/benefit levels, drafting employee letters, and tracking responses/authorizations/premium billings and benefit periods.

- Assist in development and rollout of a Corporate Wellness Program – employee communication, webpage development, employee engagement and incentive programs, Return on Investment (ROI) preparation, and monitoring.

- With the oversight and direction of their manager, partner with insurance broker to manage annual benefit renewal process including analyzing benefit options, preparing in-depth spreadsheet analyses of various plan designs options, conducting research and summarize results. 

- Lead organizational implementation of any benefit modifications and staff manual updates.

- Organize and hold annual benefit fairs and health clinics at E*Trade facilities.

- Identify and implement best practices to ensure accurate, efficient, complete and timely processing of benefits information.  This includes working closely with appropriate parties to enhance policies and procedures (e.g. working with insurance brokers, 3rd party administrators, benefit vendors, HRIS representatives, etc.).

- Monitor employee terminations and enrollments, and ensure accurate/timely monthly invoicing of all benefit vendors to payroll specialist for cost allocation and then to accounts payable for payment.

Staff Communication

- Provide customer service to employees. Conduct benefits fairs as needed.

- Counsel employees clearly and effectively face-to-face or in writing and answer questions regarding benefit programs and policies.  Provide formal benefits-related employee communication regularly.  Organize and coordinate benefit information programs.  Develop written materials to describe benefits programs and election options.

- Create communication campaign to rollout a Corporate Wellness Program: employee communication, webpage development, employee engagement and incentive programs.

- Develop and distribute annual benefit summary for staff.  Conduct annual research on benefit summary options, coordinate with appropriate parties to compile/create appealing and easy-to-read summary for staff.

- Maintain and update the benefit section on company intranet as needed, ensuring that the information posted is accurate and current.

Education/Training/Experience

- Bachelor’s degree required.

- Industry certifications and training highly desired: Professional in Human Resources (PHR), Certified Employee Benefit Specialist (CEBS), Qualified 401K Administrator (QKA),Certified Benefits Professional (CEP).

- Five plus years experience in group benefits and plan-level administration including 401(k) retirement plans. 

- Experience coordinating the annual benefit renewal and open enrollment process.

- Experience with HRIS including on-line enrollment and benefit management products required.

- Thorough knowledge of plan design and benefit contract language. Ability to interpret policies, keep records to meet legal requirements, counsel employees and solve problems.

- Solid Microsoft Office skills and exceptional Excel skills.

- Strong research and analysis skills. Able to interpret data, build sophisticated spreadsheets, summarize results and make recommendations.

- Demonstrated customer service skills - highly responsive to internal clients and their needs.

- Strong communication skills; ability to work in groups and individually to meet goals; ability to understand organizational structure and work through administrative systems.

- Pro-active.  Strong organizational skills.  Demonstrates initiative.  Able to set priorities, organize time and identify resources for projects. Takes ownership of work.  Able to work effectively in a fast-paced environment with high standards.

- Strong attention to detail and insistence on accuracy.  Develop and maintain quality control procedures to ensure accuracy and completeness of work.

- Able to confidentially maintain personal information.  Understands and respects the sensitive nature of information and manages this responsibly to ensure issues are handled discreetly, effectively and efficiently.
