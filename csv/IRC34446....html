Detailed Description

The Senior Risk Manager will be a registered associate with at least 10 years experience in the retail banking & brokerage business, preferably branch related, and familiar with sales and operational practices related to financial services support and advisory products & services. The primary function of this position will be to assist the retail management teams in ensuring proper supervisory processes and procedures are in place within groups supported per FINRA Rule 3010, Investment Advisor Act of 1940, and other regulatory rules that may come into scope based on new requirements.  This role will require staying abreast of current legal and regulatory landscape, including new laws, amended regulations and their interpretation & impact.  Further, the role will be responsible for driving analysis, dialog and structural decision-making around all risk-oriented facets of the retail business as it relates to internal procedures, firm and customer risk mitigation.  The position will work closely with the Branch Management team on process improvements that correlate to risk and operational items.   The Senior Risk Manager position will have direct managerial responsibility of the Branch Operations Managers throughout the 30 branch locations.  This position will also be involved in creating and delivering plans to improve branch sales, service and operational processes.

 

• Gain a thorough understanding of the support and services of the NRS Team, Compliance, IAD team, and our external auditing firm, Deloitte.   
• The Senior Risk Manager will be actively involved in writing the policies and procedures and periodic updates.  They must become an expert on policies and procedures for all lines of business accountable to regulatory compliance. Must have a thorough understanding of “Books and Records” for the firm (Genie/ADP).
• Provide support, training and alignment of general regulatory processes and sales practices, which include respectively, but is not limited to reviews of associate registration and licensing, KYC information, suitability of investment recommendation, client profile and risk tolerance information, investment proposals, system permissions, trade ticket/blotter reviews, external e-mail usage, record retention (electronic and hard copy), delegation memos, credit & disbursement approval levels, correspondence reviews and adherence to Written Supervisory Procedures (WSPs). 
• Conduct OSJ and site reviews & support all groups that sell ETCM products, including M.I.P and U.M.A.
• Proactively work to stay abreast of changes to regulatory supervisory requirements and work with the appropriate internal partners and business to make changes as necessary
• Deliver E*Trade Securities and E*Trade Capital Management procedural training and coaching to Managers
• Assist the Regional Managers in determining branch locations that need improvement as it relates to internal audits.
• Effectively manage a team of up to 15 Operations Managers across the country and help streamline the responsibilities across all locations.
• Leverage a strong understanding of margin as it relates to firm and customer areas of risk
• Provide SME on projects and initiatives as it relates to regulatory compliance (Sales and Service)
• Provide support and documentation for SOX and SAS 70 testing and audit reviews.
• Assure a centralized and standardized filing system for record keeping.
• Develop strong internal relationships to create  synergies with all groups that interface with the Sales and Service teams
• Help to identify operational gaps/inefficiencies and escalate accordingly
• Help to establish process improvements and efficiencies
